Explore The Olympics

The Olympics represent the pinnacle of global athletic competition, bringing together nations to celebrate excellence, unity, and the human spirit. Held every four years, the Games showcase a wide range of sports, from timeless classics like track and field to thrilling modern additions. Beyond the competition, the Olympics inspire through stories of perseverance and triumph, fostering a spirit of international competition. Whether through iconic moments, the pursuit of records, or the power of teamwork, the Olympics continue to captivate and unite audiences worldwide.


Results from the last 3 Summer Olympic Games

The United States has demonstrated consistent dominance over the last three Summer Olympics, culminating in a stellar performance at the 2024 Paris Olympics, where Team USA secured 126 medals, including 40 golds, 44 silvers, and 42 bronzes, topping the medal table. The men's and women's basketball teams secured gold, while swimming and track events yielded numerous podium finishes

At the 2016 Rio Olympics, the U.S. achieved its highest medal tally in over three decades with 121 medals, including 46 golds. Stars like Michael Phelps and Simone Biles shone brightly, with Phelps becoming the most decorated Olympian in history and Biles winning four gold medals in gymnastics

In the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, delayed to 2021 due to the pandemic, Team USA earned 113 medals—39 golds, 41 silvers, and 33 bronzes—again leading the medal count. Highlights included Caeleb Dressel's dominance in the pool and the women’s gymnastics team overcoming challenges to excel. Across these Games, the United States consistently showcased its depth and talent across diverse sports, reinforcing its position as a global leader in athletics.

At the 2024 Paris Olympics, American middle-distance runner Cole Hocker achieved an impressive victory in the men's 1500-meter final. Hocker delivered a dramatic come-from-behind performance, crossing the finish line with a time of 3:27.65, breaking the Olympic record previously set by Norway's Jakob Ingebrigtsen. His victory was a major highlight of the race, which saw his teammate Yared Nuguse also clinch a medal, finishing third. Hocker's triumph solidified his place as one of the premier middle-distance runners in the world, and it marked the 13th medal for Team USA's track and field team at the Paris Games
